Our Process
Prep is where the job is won or lost. We clean surfaces, sand, fill holes and cracks, caulk gaps, prime bare spots, and protect floors, trim, and furniture before a drop of finish paint goes on. Rushed prep is why other paint jobs look bad in two years. We don't rush prep.
We use professional-grade paints — Sherwin-Williams and Benjamin Moore are our go-tos depending on the application. We're happy to use a specific product if you have a preference. We don't cut corners with builder-grade paint.
Typically two finish coats after primer as needed. Some surfaces — particularly dark-to-light color changes or raw wood — may need additional coats. We include what's necessary to do the job right, not what's easiest.
Always. We remove all tape, drop cloths, and materials. Furniture goes back. Floors are clean. You shouldn't be able to tell we were there except for the fresh paint.
Most interior latex paints are dry to the touch in 1–2 hours and can be recoated in 4 hours. Full cure takes 2–4 weeks. We'll let you know any specific care instructions for your project before we leave.
